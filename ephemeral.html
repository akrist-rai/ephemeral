Ruhenheim — The Fall of the Convolutional Empire
⚡ 250 XP ⏱ ~45 MIN 5 CTF CHALLENGES 6 RESOURCES 94% COMPLETION
// THE QUESTION
"Dr. Tenma, the old painter asked — if a Transformer can see the whole canvas at once, why did we ever need CNNs at all?"
// THE SETUP
For years, CNNs were undisputed — ResNet, VGG, EfficientNet. Then in 2020, "An Image is Worth 16x16 Words" dropped Vision Transformers and quietly made Ruhenheim out of the old paradigm. Your job: understand why. Then prove it under pressure in the CTF Arena.
// LEARNING OBJECTIVES
✓
Understand convolution as a spatial feature extractor
✓
Explain CNN inductive biases — locality, translation equivariance
Describe self-attention and how ViT applies it to image patches
Compare data efficiency and scaling between CNNs and ViTs
Articulate when you'd still choose a CNN over a Transformer
// YOUR MISSION
Read the resources in order. Take notes. Enter the CTF Arena — 5 challenges, 20 minutes on the clock, no multiple choice. You'll be handed code blocks, model configs, and architectural traces to analyse. Derive the answer. Submit the exact flag. Three attempts per challenge before the answer is revealed. Like Johan — there are no second chances in Ruhenheim.
6 CURATED RESOURCES // READ IN ORDER
📄
An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: ViT PAPER
arxiv.org · Dosovitskiy et al. 2020 · ~20 min
The Ruhenheim paper — the document that changed everything. Skim intro and Figures 1–3. Understand patch-embedding before the math.
▶
But what is a convolution? — 3Blue1Brown VIDEO
youtube.com · 3Blue1Brown · 23 min
Even Guts studied his enemy before the fight. Build intuition for CNNs before learning what ViTs replace.
◈
ViT Explained with Code — AI Summer ARTICLE
theaisummer.com · ~12 min read
Patch tokens, positional encoding, the CLS token. Pay attention — the CTF will ask about positional encoding specifically.
📄
A ConvNet for the 2020s — ConvNeXt PAPER
arxiv.org · Liu et al. 2022 · abstract + intro only
The CNN strikes back. Like Askeladd surviving when no one expected it — modernised CNNs can match ViTs. Critical for nuance.
◈
Convolutional Networks — Stanford CS231n ARTICLE
cs231n.stanford.edu · Stanford · ~15 min read
The definitive breakdown of CNN inductive biases — locality, weight sharing, translation equivariance. Understand exactly what ViTs had to learn from scratch that CNNs get for free.
📊
ViT Benchmarks — Papers With Code LIVE DATA
paperswithcode.com · live leaderboard
Real numbers. Note where ViTs dominate (large-scale pretraining) vs where CNNs hold (small datasets). There is no painless lesson.
